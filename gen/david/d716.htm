TENTH GENERATION

730. James HENTHORN was born about 1710 in County Meath, Ireland. He died in 1776 in Martinsburg,Berkeley,VA (WV).
!Note:"Two young men, James and John Henthorn, set out from Ireland for the New World abt 1730.....In 1734, both John and James are listed in adjoining London Grove Twnshp. James married abt 1732 and became a blacksmith....The names of John, James, and James' wife, Mary, are listed in London Grove 25 Nov 1736."

!Source: "The Henthorns: A Tip of the Iceberg", Elizabeth Estlack Mullett and Joseph Henthorn. Sent to Linda Henthorn, Shari Bruce, and Elizabeth Helen Jane Estlack, summary and introduction by Norma L Henthorn and Raymond M Bell.

!Note:"James Henthorn (Isaac-1) was born 1706 in Meath County, Ireland, and died Jefferson Co, WV. He married Mary 1732. James was a colonial soldier. He fought in the French and Indian War under Capt Joseph Chapline....James was educated by a Mr. Ross in Old Castle, County Meath, Ireland. He entered Trinity College in 1724 at the age of 18. He took a BA degree from Trinity College-Dublin in 1728. James and his brother, John, came [to] the colonies about 1730 from Ireland. James became a blacksmith."

Trinity College and the son of Isaac is the same James Henthorn who is our ancestor, with only the proximity of time as evidence. Given the political climate of the time in Ireland, would a man with a college education, a sign of some status at the period come to America to become a blacksmith?

Subject: James Henthorn

BIOGRAPHY: Isaac Henthorn, and his wife, moved from England to Ireland during Cromwellian invasion/occupation in the late 1600s. They eventually had a son named James, who graduated with a BA from Trinity College Dublin when he was 18, which would be in about 1720? Many people, including some Henthorns still living near the Hamlet Henthorn in England, believe it is that James and his brother John who were the first Henthorns to arrive in the American colonies.
